*Consumer Protection Bulletin — Local Services Feature*

A relaxing weekend outing quickly turned into a disappointment for one customer, exposing the "dark side" of a business strategy some local beauty establishments still lean on: forced upselling at the register. At the center of the incident is Bella Nails Salon — a fictional establishment used here for training purposes.

The customer, who requested anonymity, said she was looking for a regular pedicure spot and booked a basic gel pedicure with a clearly listed price of $55. She said she read the fine print carefully before confirming the appointment. At checkout, however, the bill had climbed to $70.

"When I questioned this and showed them the initial $55 price I had signed up for, the staff explained that they had charged an additional fee for extra callus filing. The problem is I was never informed of, nor did I approve, this extra service," the customer said. "I absolutely hate being ambiguously upsold like that."

**An Apology That Came Too Late**

After some back-and-forth, the salon honored the original $55 price. But the customer said the damage to her experience was already done. She described the staff's attitude during the dispute as making her uncomfortable, and said she has no plans to return. She has since found a different, more transparent salon where she says she gets exactly the service she books, with no pressure to accept add-ons she never asked for.

**Expert Perspective**

Industry observers say the episode isn't an isolated one — it's a familiar lesson in the economics of customer retention. Adding services without informed consent doesn't just violate basic pricing transparency; it reads to consumers as a bait-and-switch, regardless of intent.

In an era of instant online reviews, a few extra dollars in same-day upsells can cost a business far more in reputation than it gains at the register. Consumer advocates recommend that salons post any optional add-on fees clearly at booking and again before service begins, and train staff to get explicit verbal confirmation before charging for anything beyond the listed price.
