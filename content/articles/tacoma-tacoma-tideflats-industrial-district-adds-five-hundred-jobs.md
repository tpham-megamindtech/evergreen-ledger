The update adds to a string of recent economic developments across Tacoma, part of a broader trend reshaping local balance sheets this year.

"It's a big step forward for Tacoma, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Sarah Castillo, regional economist.

Analysts point to Tacoma's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 40 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Sarah Castillo

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
