The update adds to a string of recent economic developments across Everett, part of a broader trend reshaping local balance sheets this year.

"It's a big step forward for Everett, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Robert Jackson, economic development officer.

Analysts point to Everett's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 43 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Robert Jackson

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
