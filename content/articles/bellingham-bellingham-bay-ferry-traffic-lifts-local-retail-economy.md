The update adds to a string of recent economic developments across Bellingham, part of a broader trend reshaping local balance sheets this year.

"It's a big step forward for Bellingham, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Jennifer Martin, chamber of commerce director.

Analysts point to Bellingham's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 46 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Jennifer Martin

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
