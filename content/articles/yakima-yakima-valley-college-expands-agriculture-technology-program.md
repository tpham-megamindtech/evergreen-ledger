District and campus leaders in Yakima say the change reflects months of planning and input from families, faculty, and students.

"It's a big step forward for Yakima, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Maria Reyes, college spokesperson.

The plan affects roughly 2842 students this year, with additional phases expected over the next 3 to 4 school years. Officials say funding is already in place for the first phase.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Maria Reyes

Families can find updates and opportunities to comment through the district's official channels in the weeks ahead.
