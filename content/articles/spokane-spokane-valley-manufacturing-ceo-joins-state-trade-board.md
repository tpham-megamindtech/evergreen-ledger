The announcement is the latest sign of momentum for Spokane's business community, which has drawn growing attention from investors across the Pacific Northwest.

"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said Nicole Davis, founder in Spokane.

The company now employs roughly 51 people across its Washington operations, with plans to keep hiring if growth holds through next year. Leadership says Spokane's talent pipeline was a deciding factor in choosing to expand locally rather than out of state.

> "We're proud of what this means for Spokane and the people who live here." — Nicole Davis

Company leadership says an update on hiring and expansion plans is expected before the end of the year.
