Health officials in Tacoma say the move is meant to keep pace with a fast-growing population and rising demand for local care.

"It's a big step forward for Tacoma, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Brian Silva, chief medical officer.

The expansion is expected to add roughly 31 new positions, including clinical and support staff, and comes as regional population growth pushes demand for care to record levels.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Brian Silva

Patients are encouraged to check with their provider directly for scheduling as the new services come online.
