"It's a big step forward for Walla Walla, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Chris Robinson, credit union spokesperson.

Analysts point to Walla Walla's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 49 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
