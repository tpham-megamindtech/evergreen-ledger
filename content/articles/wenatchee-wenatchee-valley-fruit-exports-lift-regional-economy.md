The update adds to a string of recent economic developments across Wenatchee, part of a broader trend reshaping local balance sheets this year.

"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said Emily Clark, regional economist in Wenatchee.

Analysts point to Wenatchee's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 50 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.

> "We're proud of what this means for Wenatchee and the people who live here." — Emily Clark

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
