"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said James Anderson, chamber of commerce director in Olympia.

Analysts point to Olympia's expanding workforce and comparatively steady cost of doing business relative to Seattle's core. The shift is expected to affect roughly 41 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Evergreen Ledger.
