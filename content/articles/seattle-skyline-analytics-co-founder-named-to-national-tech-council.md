The announcement is the latest sign of momentum for Seattle's business community, which has drawn growing attention from investors across the Pacific Northwest.

"We've put a lot of work into getting this right, and people are already noticing the difference," said Laura Ramirez, chief operating officer in Seattle.

The company now employs roughly 49 people across its Washington operations, with plans to keep hiring if growth holds through next year. Leadership says Seattle's talent pipeline was a deciding factor in choosing to expand locally rather than out of state.

> "This is exactly the kind of progress our community deserves." — Laura Ramirez

Company leadership says an update on hiring and expansion plans is expected before the end of the year.
